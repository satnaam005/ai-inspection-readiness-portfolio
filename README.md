**AI-Enabled Inspection Readiness & Risk Detection**

**Executive Summary**

Regulatory inspection outcomes are often driven by documentation gaps, inconsistent procedures, and weak quality system alignment. While these signals appear early in documentation workflows, they are rarely analyzed proactively.

This proof-of-concept leverages Natural Language Processing (NLP) to shift quality oversight from a reactive compliance exercise to a proactive, decision-support capability. This tool identifies recurring systemic execution gaps before they escalate into regulatory observations.

**Project Objective**

To demonstrate how AI can be utilized as a strategic decision-support layer within Biopharma and MedTech quality systems.
Focuses on systemic realities: Understands the crux of GMP execution and process breakdowns.
Proactive detection: Maps early risk signals to structural quality frameworks.
Human-in-the-loop: Designed to empower Quality and Product Management units, not replace human expertise.

**Technical Architecture (High-Level)**

Data Source: Multi-year FDA Inspection Observation Database, filtered and isolated for specific industry segments.
Data Engineering: Automated text pipeline designed to ingest, sanitize, and structure raw, unstructured regulatory narratives.
NLP Pipeline: Contextual analysis and feature extraction applied to identify recurring compliance themes, systemic language patterns, and structural anomalies within the text.
Risk Mapping: A proprietary heuristic framework that translates qualitative regulatory observations into standardized, high-level operational risk domains.

**High-Level Insights**

The NLP analysis revealed that execution gaps on the manufacturing floor frequently stem from foundational design gaps in procedures and specifications. The language of systemic failure is highly structured, making it identifiable and preventable through data-driven quality engineering.
